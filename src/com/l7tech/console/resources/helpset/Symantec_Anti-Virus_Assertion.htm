Using the Symantec™ Anti-Virus Assertion

 NOTE: The Administrator is responsible for installing and configuring the Symantec™ Custom Assertion package in the SecureSpan™ Gateway.

Installing and configuring the Symantec™ Custom Assertion package in the SecureSpan™ Gateway installs and enables the Symantec assertion in the SecureSpan™ Manager. The Symantec anti-virus assertion — called "Symantec Virus Scanning” in the [Assertions] tab and Policy Development window — instructs the SecureSpan Gateway to send messages and attachments to an existing Symantec Virus Scanning engine. For virus protection, the Symantec Virus Scanning engine scans all MIME (Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions) message parts of request or response messages. The anti-virus engine sends a pass or fail result to the SecureSpan Gateway. If the message is clean, then the assertion passes and the SecureSpan Gateway continues to process the policy. If the message contains a virus, then the assertion fails.

Symantec Assertion Rules

Note the following rules when using the Symantec™ Virus Scanning assertion:

  • To scan request messages, place the Symantec Virus Scanning before the routing assertion (see Configure and Edit Message Routing Assertions) in a policy

  • To scan the response messages, place the Symantec Virus Scanning after the routing assertion in a policy.

 

Add the Symantec Assertion

 

  1. Access the Policy Development window for the target service by one of two methods:

 TIP: Disable a service while constructing its policy to prevent unsecured and unauthorized access. A disabled service contains a red "X" through its left-side icon. See Manage Published Services for information.

    • Right-click the service name in the [Services] tab. A drop-down menu appears. Select Policy Assertions. Or,

    • Double-click the service in the [Services] tab.

  1. The Policy Development window appears. Add the "Symantec Virus Scanning" assertion to the policy by one of two methods:

    • Drag and drop the assertion from the [Assertions] tab into the Policy Development window, or   

    • Highlight the assertion in the [Assertions] tab and click the  (Add Assertion) button in the Assertion Tool Bar.

  1. The assertion is added to the Policy Development window. Proceed to the "Validate a Policy" section in Configure and Edit Policies.
